   Albert Einstein ( 1879 - 1955) the most recognized scientists and considered the most influential physicist of the 20th century. He is well known for his special and general theories of relativity that proposed revolutionary changes in human concepts of time, space and gravity. He stands behind probably the most famous formula E = mcÂ² . In 1921, Einstein won the Nobel Prize for Physics for explanation of the phenomenon known as the photoelectric effect. He worked on many other influential theories and projects including: the deflection of light by gravity, the quantum theory of atomic motion in solids, Brownian motion, an explanation for capillary action and much more.Throughout his life, Einstein published more than 300 scientific and 150 non-scientific papers.

Selected work:

Special Theory of Relativity

Helped to explain that time and motion are relative to their observers, as long as the speed of light remains constant and natural laws are the same throughout the universe.

General Theory of Relativity

Proposed that gravity is a curved field in the space-time continuum created by the existence of mass.

E=mc2

Demonstrates that mass and energy are the same physical entity and can be changed into each other.Concept that led to the development of nuclear energy.

Quantum Theory of Light

Proposed that light is composed of small packets of energy(photons) that have wave like properties. This theory later led to the invention of the Television).

Brownian Movement

Oservation of the zigzag movement particles in suspension, helped to prove the existence of atoms and molecules- fundamental discovery for almost every branch of science.

Manhattan Project

Research supported by the U.S., that led to the development of the atomic bomb in 1945. After realizing atomic bomb's destruction power, Einstein campaigned for a ban on nuclear weapon.
